TRAILS

Minnehaha Creek Trail — Minneapolis

A peaceful urban oasis, this riverside path winds along Minnehaha Creek beneath towering oaks and maples. Expect gentle terrain, soothing water sounds, and occasional wildlife sightings — perfect for a calm morning walk or family-friendly hike.

Battle Creek Regional Park — St. Paul

For a bit of challenge close to home, Battle Creek offers rolling hills, forested single-track, and prairie overlooks. This trail network is ideal for hikers craving variety, with twisting paths that feel surprisingly remote for being in the city.

Theodore Wirth Quaking Bog Loop — Minneapolis

Step into one of the city’s most unique ecosystems. A floating boardwalk carries you above rare plant life in a peaceful bog, surrounded by quiet woods and bird song — a short but unforgettable nature escape.

Crosby Farm Regional Park — St. Paul

Follow shaded riverside trails and explore quiet floodplain forests along the Mississippi. Smooth, wide paths make this route welcoming for casual hikers, while side trails offer a little extra adventure near the water’s edge.

Hyland Lake Loop — Bloomington

Just minutes from the metro, this looping trail circles sparkling Hyland Lake and winds through gently rolling, wooded terrain. In every season, expect wide-open views, migratory birds, and plenty of places to pause and breathe in nature.

Lebanon Hills West Loop — Eagan

A favorite for its mix of wooded trails, lakes, and peaceful marshland boardwalks, Lebanon Hills provides a true north-woods feel without the drive. Choose your distance and pace — the interconnected paths make it easy to wander and explore.
